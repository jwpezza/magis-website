01 — Mission

The world's greatest challenge is no longer finding strategic resources. It's unlocking them.

Magis is Latin for the more: doing more, for the greater good, and refusing to accept mediocrity.

It is the standard we hold ourselves to — and why governments, investors and industry trust us with decisions that shape the future.

Critical minerals are no longer a commodity question. They are a question of industrial policy, export control and national security — and governments have moved from encouraging supply to taking equity in it, financing it and contracting for its output.

But no country can onshore its way out. Minerals are where they are. The next phase of supply-chain resilience runs through the allied jurisdictions that hold the endowment — and through the harder work of making projects there financeable, governable and durable.

That is our practice. We advise governments and state-owned enterprises on unlocking the value of strategic assets. We advise investors and operators on where to commit and how to structure. Where the capital does not yet exist, we assemble it. The firm owns no mining assets.

Arrangements that are not fair do not survive, and a supply chain built on arrangements that do not survive is not a supply chain. This is not sentiment. It is the design requirement — and it is why we are trusted on both sides of the table.

We do not claim to be independent of outcomes. Few useful advisers are. We declare every interest before the work begins, and we build arrangements to outlast the transaction that created them.

01

Strategic Mining Investment Advisory

Background

A private family office with no prior exposure to mining sought an independent assessment of the sector, as geopolitical attention turned to critical minerals and security of supply.

Challenge

The question was not which asset to buy. It was whether mining warranted a place in the portfolio at all — and if so, on what basis. The client required a structured understanding of:

  • How the industry operates, and why it cycles
  • The opportunity set across the value chain — exploration, development, production, royalties and streaming
  • Capital requirements, investment horizons and risk-return characteristics at each stage
  • How each of these mapped to the family’s objectives, ticket size and risk appetite
Our role

Strategic advisory designed to build a durable investment framework rather than to recommend individual securities. The work was structured around the decisions the family would face repeatedly, not the opportunity in front of them.

Outcome

The client came away with a structured understanding of the sector and a disciplined basis for evaluating strategic resource opportunities as they arise.

02

Government Training & Capacity Building

Background

A ministry of mines in an emerging jurisdiction sought to strengthen institutional capability as it advanced development of its strategic mineral resources.

Challenge

Officials negotiating with international investors required a working understanding of how the industry actually operates — how projects are financed, how capital decides, and what a jurisdiction must do to attract long-term investment on terms that serve it.

Our role

Design and delivery of an executive programme covering:

  • The global mining industry and the project life cycle, from exploration to production
  • Project financing and capital markets
  • Mergers, acquisitions and joint venture structures
  • Commodity cycles and investment decision-making
  • The relationship between governments and investors, and how projects are positioned to attract international capital
Outcome

Participants left with a clearer understanding of the investment process, supporting more informed decision-making as the jurisdiction advances its strategic mineral resources.
